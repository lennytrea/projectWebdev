The Internet

What is the Internet?

A global network of billions of interconnected computers and devices that communicate using a shared set of rules called protocols.

Key Facts

  • Not owned by any single entity.
  • Made up of millions of LANs all linked together.
  • Devices connect via physical cables, fibre optics, or wirelessly.
  • Over 5 billion users worldwide.

A Brief History

  • 1969 - ARPANET created by the U.S. Department of Defense — the first version of the internet.
  • 1983 - TCP/IP adopted as the standard communication protocol.
  • 1991 - The World Wide Web launched, making the internet accessible to the public.
  • Today - A global infrastructure supporting communication, commerce, media, and more.

How It Works

  • Data is broken into small pieces called packets.
  • Packets travel across networks via routers and switches.
  • They are reassembled at the destination device.
  • All of this is governed by the TCP/IP protocol.

Internet vs. the Web

  • The Internet is the physical infrastructure — the network itself.
  • The World Wide Web is a service (protocol) that runs on top of it.
  • Email, online gaming, and video calls are not the web.
